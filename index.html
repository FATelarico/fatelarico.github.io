Free-access repositories to access some of my publications online.

When research findings are provided online without access fees or other obstacles, it is done so in accordance with a set of guiding principles called open access (OA). By using an open license for copyright, restrictions to copying or reusing are likewise diminished or eliminated under various forms of open access publication. While peer reviewed papers are the most common open-access content, the same approach can be used for any published research output: academic journal articles that have not undergone peer review, conference papers, dissertations, book sections, monographs, software, and visualisations.

Research projects

18. НП„Неразказаните истории на българите“ [National Program 18 - Bulgarians' untold stories]

The program supports the implementation of the policy of the Ministry of Education and Science for patriotic education, the formation of personal qualities, values, attitudes and motives, to support children's and students' full development.

[In Bulgarian]
Comparison and evaluation of different approaches to blockmodeling dynamic networks by simulations with application to Slovenian co-authorship networks

The approaches to blockmodeling dynamic networks are very useful for studying scientific collaboration as operationalised by co-authorship (due to ‘noise’, lag in publications, etc.), and it makes observing changes over time easier, which is a very important issue in the social sciences.

Project description
НП Живописна България. Български маршрути [National Project - Picturesque Bulgaria. Bulgarian routes]

The project Picturesque Bulgaria is based on the modern understanding of intercultural communication as a ‘transfer’ and a way of sharing knowledge and values; it is also based on the deep conviction that the academic field of Bulgarian studies, developed within the country and abroad, creates the most effective mediators between contexts and the most prepared ‘ambassadors’ of Bulgarian culture.
